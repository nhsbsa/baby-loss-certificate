The purpose of recording and issuing a Certificate of Baby Loss is to provide comfort and support to bereaved parents by recognising a loss.

Who is entitled

You are entitled to a Certificate of Baby Loss if you have experienced a loss under 24 weeks gestation, are at least 16 years of age, and were living in England at the time of the loss.

Initially, only those who have experienced a loss since 1 September 2018 and are currently living in England are eligible to apply for a certificate. In future, once we have assessed the demand for this service:

  • there will be no time limits to request a certificate for an historic loss, and
  • those currently living outside of England will be able request a certificate for a loss that occurred while they were living in England

How you can request a certificate

You can request a certificate online using the Certificate of Baby Loss service:

www.request-a-baby-loss-certificate.service.gov.uk

You will receive a paper certificate in the post within 14 days of us receiving your request.

Entitlements

The certificate is produced following a self-declaration by the parent.

The certificate cannot be used as evidence of identity and does not allow entitlement to any services or benefits.

How to report a change in details

If your details change from those provided for your certificate, you must submit a new request with your new details.

Once you have received the new certificate, you can destroy the old one.

Lost, damaged or incorrect certificates

If you lose or damage your certificate, or there is an error on your certificate, you must submit a new request.
