If a pregnancy ends before 24 weeks, parents can get a certificate in memory of their baby.

Parents do not have to request a certificate - it’s optional. It’s free to get a certificate.

Why we're contacting you

We've received a request for a baby loss certificate.

The main applicant has named you as the other parent of their baby. They would like to include your name on their certificate.

If you would like your name on their certificate, we’ll ask you some questions. You’ll have 7 days to give permission and confirm your details.

One certificate will be posted to the main applicant. If you would like your own certificate, you can request one.

If you decide not to add your name, the request will be cancelled. The main applicant may choose to start a new request for a certificate with only themselves shown as the parent of their baby.

About the certificate

The certificate is not a legal document. For example, it cannot be used to claim benefits.

It can show:

  • name of parents
  • relationship to baby
  • baby's name
  • baby's sex
  • date of baby loss
  • place of baby loss

The certificate will not be added to your GP record.

If parents have lost more than one pregnancy, they can apply for a separate certificate for each baby loss.
