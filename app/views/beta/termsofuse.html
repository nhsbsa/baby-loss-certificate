Certificate of Baby Loss Terms of Use

The purpose of recording and issuing a Certificate of Baby Loss is to provide comfort and support to bereaved parents by recognising a loss.

Who is entitled

You are entitled to a Certificate of Baby Loss if you:

  • have experienced a loss under 24 weeks gestation (or 28 weeks gestation if your pregnancy ended before 1 October 1992)
  • are at least 16 years of age
  • are living in England

How you can request a certificate

You can request a certificate online using the Certificate of Baby Loss service:

http://www.request-a-baby-loss-certificate.service.gov.uk/

You will receive a paper certificate in the post within 14 days of us receiving your request.

Entitlements

The certificate is produced following a self-declaration by the parent.

The certificate cannot be used as evidence of identity and does not allow entitlement to any services or benefits.

How to report a change in details

If your details change from those provided for your certificate, you must submit a new request with your new details. Once you have received the new certificate, you can destroy the old one.

Lost, damaged or incorrect certificates

If you lose or damage your certificate within three months of your initial request, you can contact us to request a duplicate certificate. If you lose or damage your certificate after three months of your initial request, or there is an error on your certificate, you must submit a new request.
